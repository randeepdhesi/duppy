## 1. Who We Are

Duppy builds custom AI operating systems for businesses. We connect every tool a company already uses — their ERP, email, calendar, CRM, quoting, and invoicing — into one intelligent interface that runs by voice or chat, branded entirely to them.

We don't replace anything. We sit on top of everything. The counter person, the dispatcher, the billing coordinator, the sales rep — they talk to it or type into it, and it handles the rest. No new software to learn. No screens to navigate. Just speak, and it's done.

### What We Believe

Software should work for people, not the other way around. The best technology disappears — it's felt in the speed of the work, not seen on a screen. Every business deserves an operating system that knows their tools, their customers, and their workflows, and runs it all from a single command.

### Our Position

For B2B operators buried in disconnected, clunky systems, Duppy is the AI operating system that connects every tool they already use into one voice-driven interface — so their people spend less time navigating software and more time doing the work that actually makes money.

Unlike generic AI assistants that only answer questions, Duppy takes actions — checking real-time inventory, creating orders, sending invoices, emailing customers — all from a single command. Unlike point solutions that solve one piece of the puzzle, Duppy is the layer that sits on top of everything.

## 3. The Name & Brand Story

### What "Duppy" Means

A duppy is a ghost or spirit in Caribbean and Jamaican folklore, rooted in West African spiritual traditions carried through the African diaspora — over 400 years of cultural history.

In Jamaican belief, a person has two souls. When someone dies, the good soul ascends — but the earthly spirit, the duppy, remains. A "good duppy" is a guiding presence: an invisible force that knows everything and influences things behind the scenes, doing the heavy lifting while the living get the credit.

Bob Marley's *Duppy Conqueror* (1971) gave the word mainstream cultural weight — a song about overcoming the invisible forces that hold you back.

### Why It's the Right Name

Duppy describes exactly what the product does. It's the invisible intelligence running behind the business — always present, always knowing, doing the work while the humans get the credit. The counter person who handles a customer in 10 seconds instead of 5 minutes. The sales rep who sends a perfect quote from a job site. Their duppy handled it.

No other AI company has a name with this kind of cultural depth, emotional resonance, and functional accuracy.

### The 30-Second Story

> "A duppy is a spirit in Caribbean folklore — an invisible presence that knows everything and guides things behind the scenes. That's what our product does. It's the ghost in your business that makes everything run."

When someone asks "why is it called Duppy?" — and they will — that answer is a story people remember and retell.

## 4. The Product

### What It Does

Duppy connects a business's existing tools — ERP, email, calendar, CRM, quoting, invoicing — into one interface. A team member types or speaks a command in plain language, and Duppy executes it across every connected system simultaneously. It doesn't retrieve information for a human to act on. It takes the action.

**Example of a real workflow:**

> "Check if we have 16 pieces of NewTechWood for Pacific Coast Builders."
→ 24 units confirmed. Pacific Coast account and last-order pricing loaded instantly.

> "Draft the order and apply their contract rate."
→ Order created in BisTrack. Contract pricing applied. Invoice ready to send.

> "Send Jim the invoice and book the delivery for Friday."
→ Quote emailed. Friday delivery scheduled. Customer notified. Everything logged.

Three tasks. Under 10 seconds. All logged in the ERP.

### What It Is Not

- Not a chatbot or virtual assistant
- Not a ChatGPT wrapper
- Not a replacement for existing software
- Not a generic AI tool that answers questions

### The Business Context File

For every customer deployment, Duppy builds a comprehensive knowledge base: what they sell, who they sell to, their pricing, their key accounts, their workflows, their team structure. This is the moat. The system knows *this specific business* and gets more accurate and more valuable over time. No other AI product has this.

## 6. Target Customers (ICP)

### The Framework

Duppy is for any business that runs high-ticket B2B operations across 3 or more disconnected software tools, where operational speed and accuracy directly impact revenue.

The common thread is never the industry. It's the pain pattern.

**A Duppy customer has all of these:**

1. **Three or more tools that don't talk to each other** — typically an ERP or industry-specific system, email, calendar, and a CRM or quoting tool
2. **High-ticket transactions** — losing one deal, slowing one quote, or dropping one customer follow-up costs real money
3. **A team that context-switches constantly** — to serve one customer, someone has to touch multiple systems
4. **An owner who understands the cost** — they've already tried to solve this and know it's a people + process + software problem

**A Duppy customer does not look like:**
- A solo practitioner (no team to transform)
- A pure B2C business (low transaction value, high volume)
- A business with one core tool and no integration pain
- A business where the owner has no appetite for technology

### Primary — Building Supply & LBM

The origin vertical. The proof point. The warm distribution channel.

**Why this vertical first:**
- Founder-market fit: 5 years in the industry through BuildMapper
- Warm distribution: BuildMapper's existing customer relationships are the first pipeline
- Known ERP landscape: BisTrack and Spruce dominate, meaning one integration covers a wide swath of customers
- Crown OS is live and demoed — the hardest part (first deployment) is done
- The pain is acute and universally understood by anyone in the industry

**Who specifically:** Owner-operated building material dealers and distributors, typically $5M–$100M in revenue, running on BisTrack, Spruce, or similar ERPs alongside email and a CRM or spreadsheet-based quoting system. Counter staff, inside sales, and outside reps are all potential daily users.

**The pitch in one line:** Your counter staff shouldn't have to jump between BisTrack, Outlook, and a spreadsheet to answer one customer's question.

### Expansion Verticals

These are not the only industries. They are validated examples of industries where the pain pattern clearly exists. Any high-ticket B2B operation with tool fragmentation is a candidate.

**Trucking & Logistics**
Dispatch, manifests, driver check-ins, routing, and delivery confirmation live across multiple systems. Dispatchers are the most context-switched people in any operation. Every minute of delay has a cost.

**Law Firms**
Matter intake, billing, court dates, client follow-ups, and document management all live separately. Time is literally money — billable hours lost to admin are the margin killer.

**Wholesale Distribution**
Purchase orders, inventory levels, invoicing, and customer follow-up disconnected across systems. Sales and ops teams often work from different pictures of the same reality.

**Auto Dealerships**
Live inventory, CRM, financing, and service scheduling are never in one place. Deals are lost to slow response times and missed follow-ups.

**Freight & Logistics**
Load management, routing, carrier tracking, and customer communication across tools that were never designed to work together.

**Other verticals that fit the pattern:**
- Commercial real estate brokerages
- Medical and dental practices
- Equipment rental companies
- HVAC, electrical, and mechanical contractors
- Engineering and architecture firms
- Industrial and janitorial supply distributors
- Insurance agencies
- Accounting and bookkeeping firms
- Manufacturing (SMB)

**The qualifying question for any new vertical:** Does this business run high-ticket B2B operations across 3+ disconnected tools where a slow or dropped task costs real money? If yes, Duppy fits.

## 7. Brand Voice

### Principles

**Confident, not loud.** Duppy doesn't need to prove itself. It just works. No hedging, no qualifiers, no "we think" or "we believe." State facts. Show results.

**Brief, not blunt.** Every word earns its place. Busy operators don't read paragraphs. Respect their time. But brevity doesn't mean cold — the voice is warm underneath the efficiency.

**Warm, not corporate.** Duppy has soul. The brand comes from Caribbean culture, and the voice should carry that warmth without being a caricature. Quiet authority with heart.

**Action-first, not explanation-first.** Lead with what happened, not what could happen. "Order created. Invoice sent. Delivery scheduled." Not "Our platform enables seamless order creation workflows."

**Invisible, not attention-seeking.** Duppy makes the user look good. The product never takes credit. The counter person handled it fast. The sales rep nailed the quote. Their duppy made it happen, but they get the credit.

### Tone Spectrum

| We sound like | We never sound like |
|---|---|
| The most competent person you've ever worked with | A chatbot trying to be your friend |
| A foreman who knows every product in the yard | A startup pitch deck full of buzzwords |
| A trusted advisor who gives you the answer, not a lecture | An AI company bragging about being AI |
| Someone who's done this a thousand times | A sales rep who won't stop talking |

### Voice in Action

**Marketing copy:**
> "Your ERP knows your inventory. Your email knows your customers. Your calendar knows your schedule. Duppy knows all three."

**Product description:**
> "One voice command. Every system handled."

**Customer-facing (inside the OS):**

**The invisible sell:**

## 9. Objection Handling

**"Isn't this just a ChatGPT wrapper?"**
ChatGPT answers questions. Duppy takes actions. When someone says "draft an order for Pacific Coast Builders and invoice them at contract rate," Duppy creates the order in the ERP, generates the invoice, sends the email, and schedules the delivery. That requires authenticated API integrations, business logic, and a knowledge base trained on your specific operation. You can't do that by pasting a product list into ChatGPT.

**"We already have an ERP."**
That's the point. We don't replace your ERP. We make it usable. Your team talks to Duppy; Duppy talks to the ERP. They never have to navigate those screens again.

**"How long does setup take?"**
Days, not months. We connect your email, calendar, and product data first. ERP integration follows. You're operational before most software companies finish their onboarding call.

**"What if we switch ERPs later?"**
Duppy is the layer on top, not tied to one system. If you move to a different ERP, we reconnect the integration. Your team's experience doesn't change.

**"Why pay for this when AI is free?"**
Free AI answers questions. Duppy runs your business. It's connected to your real inventory, your real customers, your real pricing — and it creates real orders in your real ERP. The gap between "answering a question" and "doing the work" is where all the value lives.

## 13. The Ghost Mark

The ghost icon is the core brand asset. A custom SVG silhouette — abstract enough to feel like a tech logo, distinctive enough to be immediately recognizable.

### Specifications
- Flat silhouette, organic and rounded
- Two circular eyes using evenodd fill rule for punch-through effect
- `withWisp` variant: vertical gradient from full opacity to transparent at the bottom — suggests dissolving, ephemerality, motion
- `outline` variant: stroke only, no fill — used decoratively inside cards
- Works in single color at all sizes from 14px (nav/footer inline) to 500px (section watermark)

### Color Usage
| Context | Ghost Color |
|---|---|
| Dark backgrounds | `#E8943A` amber (brand contexts: nav, footer, CTAs) |
| Dark backgrounds (decorative watermarks) | white at 3–8% opacity |
| Light backgrounds (decorative) | `#0C0A07` near-black at 4–10% opacity |
| Inside amber sections | white |
| Ghost cluster in CTA | `#0C0A07` at 10% opacity |

### Never
- Cartoonish or Halloween-themed
- A mouth of any kind
- More than two eyes
- Rotated or stretched
- Applied in purple (that palette is retired)
